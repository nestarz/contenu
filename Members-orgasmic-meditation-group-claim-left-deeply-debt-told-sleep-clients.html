Members of orgasmic meditation group where men stimulated women for 45 mins every day claim they were left deeply in debt and told to have sex to attract potential clients in a 'kind of prostitution ring'

  • OneTaste was founded in 2004 by Nicole Daedone and promotes wellness through orgasmic meditation
  • Members who lived in the group's communal homes would be stimulated three times a day for 15 minutes each time, former members told Bloomberg 
  • Ruwan Meepagala said he left the group owing $30,000 on his credit cards from classes and sessions
  • He also claimed that he was told to sleep with an older female in order to get her to sign up for classes  
  • 'Many of the former staffers and community members say OneTaste resembled a kind of prostitution ring that exploited trauma victims,' reports Bloomberg
  • OneTaste says the article paints the company in a false light, hand-picking the sensational allegations of a few while ignoring thousands of happy customers

Former members of OneTaste, a company that teaches and researches orgasmic meditation, are making shocking claims about their time with the group.

Members live or stay in communal homes and are 'stimulated' three times a day for 15 minutes, with packages costing up to $60,000.

But multiple men and women have come forward saying they were left in debt after joining the wellness company and told to engage in sexual relations with potential clients to close sales.

They told Bloomberg Businessweek that they were encouraged to take expensive classes and shunned by members of the community when they did not choose to participate in the sessions.  

OneTaste tells DailyMail.com that the Bloomberg article paints the company in a false light, hand-picking the sensational allegations of a few while ignoring thousands of satisfied and happy customers. 

Moreover, a spokesperson for the group states that all of the allegations are more than two years old from before OneTaste transformed, under new ownership and leadership, into a traditional company with strong corporate governance, clear-cut sales policies and practices, and strict HR policies.

Founder: OneTaste was founded in 2004 by Nicole Daedone (above) and promotes wellness through orgasmic meditation

The act: Members who lived in the group's communal homes would be stimulated three times a day for 15 minutes each time former members told Bloomberg (a scene of orgasmic meditation from the film Sleeping With Friends)

OneTaste students currently pay $499 for a weekend course, $4,000 for a retreat, $12,000 for a spot in the coaching program, and $16,000 for intensives, with an all-inclusive option also offered for $60,000. 

Nicole Daedone, 50, who founded the company in 2004, stepped away from teaching classes for OneTaste recently. 

According to 2013 Gawker story, she was introduced at one event as the 'Jimi Hendrix of stroking.'

She did not comment on the article.  

OneTaste has been featured everywhere fromThe New York Times to GOOP, which had an interview with Daedone.

She revealed she almost chose to become a celibate Buddhist in that interview before extolling the virtues of her program.

'OM changes the way we respond to sensation; it changes our brain. It strengthens the parasympathetic nervous system ("rest and digest") as opposed to the sympathetic nervous system ("fight or flight"),' said Daedone in that interview. 

'And it affects our metabolism, heart rate, blood pressure, respiration, and brain chemistry, and brings about a state of extended deep relaxation. Similar to other mindfulness practices like meditation and yoga, these changes make it possible to develop attention and access flow states—the ability to be "in the zone."'

Those claims were part of the attraction for students including Ruwan Meepagala, who said he left the group owing $30,000 on his credit cards from classes and sessions. 

He said he was once chided when he pointed out that it had been two months since his last paycheck.

Ruwan Meepagala said he left the group owing $30,000 on his credit cards from classes and sessions

Meepagala also said that he was told to have sex with an older woman before selling her classes, and said he heard women do the same when trying to sell or get men to pay for their classes.

'A lot of women would be like, "I’m going to hook this guy for money,"' said Meepagala. 

'They would brag about it.'  

'This is a wholly false and disgusting allegation,' said a spokesperson. 

'OneTaste never ordered anyone to have sex with anyone else nor did it ever have the power to do so. '

The company also addressed Meepagala's claims directly, stating: 'Ruwan came to OneTaste for the lifestyle community to explore his sexuality as a full-time, live-in student. He performed some work for a short time period, but, contrary to his expectations, he was not compensated for personal activities such as attending classes, being coached and other things that did not qualify as work. 

'While he falsely alleges manipulation, Bloomberg failed to point out that he has started a business whose model appears to be teaching men to manipulate women. '

Bloomberg spoke with 16 individuals who at one point had ties to the group for their story, some of whom spoke anonymously for fear of retribution or violating documents they had signed in the past. 

'Many of the former staffers and community members say OneTaste resembled a kind of prostitution ring—one that exploited trauma victims and others searching for healing,' reports the story. 

'In their experience, the company used flirtation and sex to lure emotionally vulnerable targets. It taught employees to work for free or cheap to show devotion. And managers frequently ordered staffers to have sex or OM with each other or with customers.'

The company refuted claims they urged staff to use sex to sell their product. They also said Meepagala now teaches 'pickup-artistry-esque techniques and isn’t a moral authority'. 

Allegation: 'Many of the former staffers and community members say OneTaste resembled a kind of prostitution ring that exploited trauma victims,' reports Bloomberg (Daedone above)

Michal, a woman who went so far as to marry a man at the suggestion of OneTaste she claims, left after she and her husband had spent $150,000 on the group.

' The deeper I went, the more courses I did, the more I worked for them, the closer I got to Nicole - I knew I was doing something that later would be very difficult to unravel,' said Michal. '

'I knew I was losing control. In OneTaste, I’d done that again and again and again.'

Michael also detailed life at the OM house when she was working as a sales person.

'Every morning at around 7 a.m., staff convened for two rounds of OM, switching partners midway. Then came an AA-inspired "fear inventory," writing out and sharing their worries with a partner,' reports Bloomberg.

'Former staffers say they were encouraged to report to management if they heard others express doubts about OneTaste. They all went to Bikram yoga, cooked, cleaned, then spent several hours making sales calls around a table, tracking their progress with Salesforce.com. After an afternoon round of OM, they left to run the evening’s public session.'

Sales made Michael $200 or $300  each month if she was lucky she said in the interview. 

'I lost my understanding of money,' said Michal. 

'There was a lot of psychological manipulation. This is an organization that really preys on people’s weaknesses.'

Senior officials in the company also deny that the company is a 'cult', but CEO Joanna Van Vleck admits some people may use that term when something 'changes their internal perspective'. 

She also insisted that the company has changed their policies to stop customers from feeling pressured into taking on debt. 

'The overarching thing is, orgasm is part of wellness,' she told Bloomberg.
